A 'Permission for Commercial Operation' (PfCO) is awarded by the Civil Aviation Authority after a person has proven, through written and practical examination, that they are competent enough to operate a commercial drone. Drones are remarkably sturdy, but most will struggle to fly reliably in winds above 21mph. In addition, propulsion comes from individual electric motors exposed beneath each propeller blade, and therefore most drones cannot operate in rain, sleet or thick fog due to the risk of moisture. Because the weather is sometimes unpredictable, a degree of flexibility is always an advantage when scheduling flights.
